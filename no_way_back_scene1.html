No Way Back: The Hotel Room Game

Chapter One: The Knock

The hallway smells like old carpet and disinfectant. Room 312 is at the far end — the door with the peeling brass number and the flickering sconce above it. You hesitate with your fist raised, knuckles just shy of the wood. Then, you knock.

The door opens faster than you expect. No chain. No hesitation. Just her — framed in the soft amber light of the room behind her. She’s barefoot, holding a half-empty glass of something dark. The case worker. The woman you swore you’d never see again.

“Detective,” she says, like a question she’s already answered. Her voice is low and calm, but there’s that edge. You remember that edge. The kind that cuts in all directions.

You step in. She doesn’t move to stop you. The door clicks shut behind you, and suddenly, it’s just you and her. The silence fills every inch of the room — and yet it’s louder than anything you heard today at the precinct.

“I need to know why you called me,” you say, finally. Her smile doesn’t reach her eyes. “You always need something, don’t you?”
